SOLAR SYSTEM

The solar system is a wondrous collection of celestial objects, all orbiting our Sun. Here's a comprehensive look at its key features and some fascinating facts:

Key Features:

  • The Sun: The central star and the main source of energy for the solar system. It makes up 99.86% of the total mass of the solar system.
  • Dwarf Planets: Pluto, Eris, Haumea, Makemake, and Ceres are some of the recognized dwarf planets in our solar system.
  • Moons: There are over 200 known moons orbiting the planets and dwarf planets, with notable ones including Earth's Moon, Jupiter's Ganymede, and Saturn's Titan.
  • Asteroids: Mostly found in the asteroid belt between Mars and Jupiter, these are rocky remnants from the early solar system.

Interesting Facts:

  • Age: The solar system is about 4.6 billion years old.
  • Solar Orbit: It takes about 225-250 million years for the solar system to complete one orbit around the Milky Way's center.
  • Jupiter's Influence: Jupiter's massive gravity can affect the orbits of other objects in the solar system, including asteroids and comets.
