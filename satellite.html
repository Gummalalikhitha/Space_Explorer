SATELLITE

Satellites are fascinating objects that have revolutionized how we observe and interact with our planet and the universe.

Key Features:

  • Low Earth Orbit (LEO): Between 160 and 2,000 kilometers above Earth. Common for imaging and communication satellites.
  • Medium Earth Orbit (MEO): Between 2,000 and 35,786 kilometers. Often used for navigation satellites like GPS.
  • Geostationary Orbit (GEO): At about 35,786 kilometers, where the satellite orbits Earth at the same rate the planet rotates, appearing stationary over a specific location. Used for weather and communication satellites

Interesting Facts:

  • First Satellite: Sputnik 1, launched by the Soviet Union in 1957, was the first artificial satellite to orbit Earth.
  • Global Positioning System (GPS): A network of at least 24 satellites provides accurate location and timing information globally.
  • Weather Satellites: Satellites like GOES and METEOSAT provide critical data for weather forecasting and monitoring natural disasters.
  • Imaging and Remote Sensing: Satellites like Landsat and Sentinel capture high-resolution images of Earth's surface, aiding in environmental monitoring, agriculture, and urban planning.
