sun

The Sun is our closest star and the center of our solar system. It's a hot ball of glowing gases that has captivated humanity for centuries.

Key Features:

  • Composition: The Sun is primarily composed of hydrogen (about 75%) and helium (about 24%), with trace amounts of other elements like oxygen, carbon, neon, and iron.
  • Temperature: The core temperature can reach about 15 million degrees Celsius, while the surface (photosphere) is about 5,500 degrees Celsius.
  • Energy Production: Through the process of nuclear fusion, the Sun converts hydrogen into helium, releasing vast amounts of energy in the form of light and heat.

Interesting Facts:

  • Size: The Sun's diameter is about 1.39 million kilometers, making it 109 times wider than Earth
  • Mass: It contains 99.86% of the total mass of the solar system.
  • Light Speed: Light from the Sun takes approximately 8 minutes and 20 seconds to reach Earth.
  • Life Cycle: The Sun is about 4.6 billion years old and is expected to shine for another 5 billion years before evolving into a red giant and eventually a white dwarf.
