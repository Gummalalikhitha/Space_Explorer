MOON

The Moon is a fascinating celestial body and Earth's only natural satellite. Here is some detailed content about the Moon

Key Features:

  • Diameter: About 3,474 km (about 27% of Earth's diameter)
  • Orbital Period: About 27.3 Earth days
  • Day Length: About 29.5 Earth days (same as its orbital period, resulting in the same side always facing Earth)
  • Surface Temperature: Ranges from -173°C at night to 127°C during the day

Interesting Facts:

  • Tidal Locking: The Moon is tidally locked to Earth, meaning the same side always faces our planet.
  • Lunar Phases: The Moon goes through a cycle of phases, from new moon to full moon and back, every 29.5 days.
  • Exploration: The first manned Moon landing was in 1969 during NASA's Apollo 11 mission. A total of 12 astronauts have walked on the Moon.
  • No Atmosphere: Due to its weak gravity, the Moon cannot hold onto an atmosphere, leading to extreme temperature variations.
  • Formation: The most widely accepted theory is that the Moon formed from the debris left after a Mars-sized body collided with Earth about 4.5 billion years ago.
