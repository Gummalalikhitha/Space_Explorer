ROCKET

Rockets are incredible vehicles designed to travel through the atmosphere and into space. They play a vital role in space exploration, satellite deployment, and even potential interplanetary travel.

Key Features:

  • Propulsion System: The heart of a rocket, responsible for generating thrust to propel the rocket forward. It includes engines that burn fuel to produce high-speed exhaust gases.
  • Stages: Rockets are often built in stages that detach after their fuel is exhausted. Each stage helps reduce weight and increase efficiency as the rocket ascends.
  • Fuel Tanks: These store the rocket's propellant, which can be either liquid or solid. The choice of fuel affects the rocket's performance and design.

Interesting Facts:

  • History: The first successful liquid-fueled rocket was launched by Robert Goddard in 1926.
  • Saturn V: The Saturn V rocket, used during the Apollo missions, remains the most powerful rocket ever built, capable of sending astronauts to the Moon.
  • Sound and Heat: Rockets produce intense sound and heat during liftoff. The noise can reach levels of 200 decibels, and the exhaust gases can reach temperatures of over 3,000 degrees Celsius
