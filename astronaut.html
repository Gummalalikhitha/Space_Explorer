ASTRONAUT

Astronauts are highly trained individuals who travel into space to conduct research, perform experiments, and explore the cosmos.

Key Features:

  • Training: Astronauts undergo rigorous training, including physical fitness, survival skills, spacecraft systems, and scientific research.
  • Skills: They must possess a wide range of skills, including problem-solving, teamwork, communication, and technical expertise.
  • Medical Fitness: Astronauts need to be in excellent health and pass stringent medical examinations.

Interesting Facts:

  • First Human in Space: Yuri Gagarin, a Soviet cosmonaut, was the first human to journey into outer space in 1961.
  • First Moon Landing: In 1969, Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin became the first humans to walk on the Moon during NASA's Apollo 11 mission.
  • Spacewalks: Also known as Extravehicular Activity (EVA), spacewalks allow astronauts to perform tasks outside their spacecraft. The first spacewalk was conducted by Alexei Leonov in 1965.
  • International Crew: Astronauts come from various countries and often work together on missions, especially on the International Space Station (ISS).
